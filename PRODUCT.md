# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Artists, labels, and client teams use True North to manage music distribution, rights, release support, and related platform requests. In the helpdesk, they are usually trying to resolve a specific issue quickly, confirm a process, or find the right next step before contacting support.

## Product Purpose

True North gives music clients a clear path through distribution, onboarding, rights management, platform forms, and support workflows. The helpdesk exists to reduce repeated support questions, make operational guidance easy to find, and keep clients moving without replacing the existing support forms page.

## Brand Personality

Clear, calm, expert. The interface should feel confident and direct, with enough True North identity to feel owned, but not so much decoration that it distracts from reading or support tasks.

## Anti-references

Avoid the current starfield heaviness on support surfaces. Avoid generic documentation templates, over-decorated SaaS cards, gradient text, glass-heavy panels, and anything that feels like a clone of Freshdesk rather than a True North helpdesk.

## Design Principles

- Search first: the fastest path to a useful answer should be obvious.
- Readability over spectacle: support articles need calm surfaces, strong contrast, and predictable structure.
- Owned but restrained: use True North pink as a functional accent, not as decoration everywhere.
- Preserve existing workflows: the new helpdesk adds article guidance without replacing `/support`.
- Design for maintenance: content should be editable through Supabase-backed admin tools.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic compliance is the floor: readable contrast, keyboard focus states, semantic headings, responsive layouts, and reduced-motion behavior for non-essential animation.
